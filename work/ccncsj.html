CCNC-SJ

CCNC–SJ is the Chinese Canadian National Council – for Social Justice. The CCNC-SJ organization serves to educate, provide awareness, and tools on anti-Asian racism, specifically racism fueled by COVID-19. They work to engage and advocate for racial justice in Canada.

I was tasked to help deliver an improved update of their current website. Improvements including visual identity, accessibility, consistency, and recall. Also to make room for upcoming content including the history of anti-racist resilience of the Asian community, and an educational toolkit provided by teachers who were hired as consultants meant to help people respond effectively to racism.


Reorganize to optimize goals

During the initial stages I focused on reorganizing the order of content on the website. It began with focusing on user stories (Victim, Emotional, and Informational users) to shuffling the site content based on the goal of the directors, which is to: push their campaigns, provide access to traditional and non-traditional media to their work, and access to their educational tools on anti-racism. The old site had a lot of page bloat and was difficult to recall, and the structure felt inconsistent. Learning about anti-racism should be a part of everyone’s lifestyle, so I focused on making the website as clean, consistent, and accessible as possible - for the boomers, the zoomers, the millennials, and hopefully the generations coming. As a result came a site map with a few sectional changes.

Wireframes

When it came to developing the wireframes, this brought more understanding to myself and the CCNC-SJ team as to what structure works best for the users and for the goals of the directors. Initial iterations had a lot less content, limited information on the landing page - solely campaign information - as I was focusing on simplicity and access. After the first interation, I learned cleanliness doesn’t always equate to access, especially if the purpose of the site is to provide tools and resources to encourage anti-racist advocacy.

The final product focused on keeping the campaigns and traditional media on the landing page - this is to drive their goals for organic campaign engagement and to provide a quick route for traditional media. I designed a structure for their campaign pages - #FaceRace and Stop the Spread - so there will be a section to house extra information and several sub-pages to be added.

Given this site will also house plenty of cited information and curated sources, we added a resources page to the global navigation to house links, sources, and partner sections such as the COVID-10 Racism Reporting Tool.
