FM28.co

Celina reached out to me in the summer of 2019 with her idea of creating an artist collective based out of LA, operating globally. She wanted to curate a collective of multifaceted artists and entrepreneurs. This site is meant as a vessel for those interested in joining and representing the FM28 community.

Sketches & Wireframes

The information on this site is contained and minimal, so the design had to reflect that while using visuals to fill up the space. The landing page was pretty straight forward – navigation, logo, footer... however for the integral pages such as Submissions and Street Team, I was able to explore a few options. I also had to keep asking myself, how many images is too many? Does that become a barrier for the user when they fill out forms? Is that even useful for people? Given the site was very limited in info and assets, I had to improvise.

What made it fun is that each page was encouraged to have its own differences such as visual composition, the type of media used, and the way images looked. For the Street Team page, I wanted to involve something humanly yet anarchic (not pop-up windows!), high energy, and rebellious, which is why we ended up going with a black & white video background of a riot.

SquareSpace as the CMS

We went with SquareSpace as a CMS as it was simple to integrate forms, third party API’s, updating content blocks, and injecting custom CSS. We worked together back-and-forth on several layout options – we went with what we currently have as the colour scheme, a high energy video of a riot from Ukraine, and early 2000’s spam windows as it aligned with the look and feel of cyber-artsy apocalyptic propaganda FM28 wanted to embrace. We also recognized we didn't want to basically image-vomit on our users unless it actually were helpful. For this project, it is entirely decorative and meant to close up any extra space.


Different assets for each page

All kinds of visuals were instrumental for the site. Throughout the course our dialogue had evolved from "use as many images as possible!" to "let's keep it minimum so it doesn't overwhelm people."

All of the assets were designed by myself, with exception of the riot video on the Street Team page. All moving images on the site are GIFs as they perform faster, easier to loop, and run upon page load.
