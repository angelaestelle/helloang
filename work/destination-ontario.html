Work About me Project: Destination Ontario

About the project

Redesigning the website for — formerly OntarioTravel.net, now, Destination Ontario. It has been over a decade since their last refresh so we began from scratch, starting with their component library.

The Challenge

The Government of Ontario needed a full refresh on their destination site. With no updated branding or any new visual elements to leverage, we were tasked to recreate the entire website starting with building their design system on a tight launch for Q1 2021.

Why this is happening

  • Planning didn’t account for COVID to affect tourism
  • Many locals were exploring local tourist spots
  • Timeline too tight to incorporate branding separately

Stakeholder input

Since this project began with missing preliminary elements (like branding), our initial meetings with the client included two directions for them to choose from. The client would provide feedback for each direction in order to narrow the options. These directions would help guide our decisions from a branding perspective once we begin designing the UI.

Insights

Accessibility first

High contrast, passes WCAG AAA standards. This would mean any colours behind text would either be very light or extremely deep and rich in tone.

Reflects Canadian nature

Destinario Ontario has a large, wide range of photography in their image bank. We used our favourite and most iconic imagery to guide our choices for palette options.

Wide palette

The last site refresh was over a decade ago. In order to meet all of its content needs and a broad range of use case possibilities, a wide range of colours is necessary.

Worst-case tested

We had to make sure long names such as Kawarthas, Northumberland and Midland-Penetanguishene would not break.

Inspirational

It’s a strange time to relaunch a tourism site during a pandemic. Because we had many locals going to local tourist destinations, we wanted to inspire others to do so as well.

Long-lasting & flexible

DO’s visual identity should be a healthy balance between editorial-like (to create inspiration) and familiar (for longevity).

Competitive Review

We looked at a handful of sites that reflected familiar, friendly, and nature-loving elements and sites that had inspirational, editorial qualities. These helped us discover visual cues that would guide the look and feel of blending both.

Visual Requirements

  • High contrast and legibility
  • Large spaces for photography
  • Lots of white space for main content area
  • No type on imagery
  • Wide leading on body text for easy scannability

Style Tiles

We created two style tiles, one each for Mosaic and Paddle, to explore the direction with more context now that we’ve defined our requirements. This allowed us to show the client what is possible for either route, without getting too caught up in the technical specs.

Defining the UI

We built responsive mockups - developing mobile first - showing key content pages of the Destination website – featured city, city, and region pages. These mockups demonstrated the interface’s adaptability across mobile and desktop screens. We designed our components organized by section following the Zurb Foundation framework.
